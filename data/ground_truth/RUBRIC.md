# HireLens Ground-Truth Scoring Rubric (Phase 5.1)

**Every rater uses THIS rubric.** Without a shared anchored scale, averaging rater
scores is meaningless. Score each resume/JD pair **0–100** for *fit*, using the
anchor points below. Rate independently and blind — do not discuss or view other
raters' scores before submitting your own.

## What "fit" means
How well this candidate's resume matches THIS job description, weighing (roughly,
in this priority order): **required skills present**, **relevant experience /
years**, **domain/education alignment**. You are judging fit for *this* role, not
the candidate's general quality.

## Anchor points (interpolate between them)

| Score | Meaning |
|------:|---------|
| **90–100** | Excellent fit. Nearly all required skills present, clearly relevant experience at the right level, no meaningful gaps. You'd shortlist without hesitation. |
| **~80** | Strong fit. Most required skills present, relevant experience, minor gaps that wouldn't block an interview. |
| **~65** | Good-but-partial fit. Core skills present but some required ones missing, or experience slightly light/adjacent. Worth a look with reservations. |
| **~50** | Borderline. Roughly half the requirements met; transferable-but-nonexact skills, career-switcher, or seniority mismatch. Genuine coin-flip. |
| **~35** | Weak fit. Some superficial overlap but most requirements unmet or experience clearly misaligned. |
| **~20** | Poor fit. Little real overlap; a few coincidental keyword matches at most. |
| **0–10** | No fit. Different field entirely. |

## Rules for raters
- **Rate blind and independently.** Do not view others' sheets first.
- **Always write a one-line justification.** State the 1–2 factors that drove your
  score (e.g. "has Python+SQL but 0 of the 3 required cloud skills; 2 yrs vs 5
  required"). This is used to reconcile disagreements and appears in the report.
- **Use the whole scale.** If everything clusters at 70–80 you are not
  discriminating; borderline cases especially should pull toward the middle.
- **Don't overthink precision.** The reconciliation step measures your agreement;
  an honest 55 is better than a falsely-precise 57.
